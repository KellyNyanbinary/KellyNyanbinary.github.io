“I Left Because I Love the Sky”

Mei writes a reflective post—all serif type, generous whitespace, and black-on-ivory minimalism straight out of early Medium.

I didn’t leave Adobe because I hated the cloud. I left because I love the sky. We used to build the tools that people created with. Then those tools began asking for permission. Subscriptions made sense on paper; they didn’t make sense for the soul.

History will call it a business shift. I’ll remember it as the moment creativity learned to wait for confirmation. When a person is forced to ping a server before painting, something essential gets rate-limited.

Platform researchers like Xiyin Tang describe this as platform capitalism reshaping labor. Subscription-economy scholars Renu Verma, Kaveri Khound, and Vagish Mishra argue that younger creators value access over ownership. I’m somewhere in the middle—I value access when it’s mutual, not conditional.
