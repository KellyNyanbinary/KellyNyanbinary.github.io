Cafeteria, Later that week

Countdown

The cafeteria hummed with the usual chatter. A few engineers scrolled through Hacker News. Mei sat with Rina, a UX researcher.

Lunch Transcript

"Are we really doing this?" Mei asked.

Rina poked at her salad. "They've wanted recurring revenue for years. Finance probably pushed hard this time."

"It's not just revenue. It's access. You lose internet and suddenly the software forgets you paid for it. We're not buying software anymore, we're renting functionality," Mei says, echoing the thesis of The End of Ownership.

Rina shrugged. "They'll say ninety days is long enough."

Mei didn't answer. Ninety days suddenly sounds like a countdown, not a grace period.
