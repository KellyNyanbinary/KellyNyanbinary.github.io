Oakland, Months Later

Aurora Paint, Perpetual

A warehouse loft full of solder smoke and synth playlists becomes the HQ for a scrappy GPLv3 alternative.

The workspace smelled like burnt coffee and solder. Miguel typed quickly, his foot tapping.

"We're calling it Aurora Paint," he said without looking up.

Mei sat beside him, opening her laptop. "Version control?"

"GitLab. Self-hosted."1

"What's the license?"

"GPLv3," Miguel said. "Always free."2

Mei nodded. The word felt soft. "Perpetual."

They worked quietly. It was a simple life: bug reports, community feedback, occasional donations. Her code felt lighter now: no locks, no timers, no license validation scripts. Just tools.

Homepage Copy

"Aurora Paint exists because art should not expire. Built by those who once wrote code for locks, now writing keys instead." The copy riffs on real-world manifestos like the Graphic Artists Guild list of free alternatives.
